# Ad Frameworks (distilled for prompt use)

Distilled from the Triple Hook Playbook, the 30 Ad Archetype Master, the Ultimate Content
Cheat Sheet, and the False Beliefs guide. This is the strategy layer for generating video ad
concepts. Injected into concept/breakdown prompts; keep it tight.

## Core principle
The creative IS the algorithm. Modern Meta rewards **creative/narrative diversity** over
targeting. Win by testing several *distinct angles*, each destroying a specific false belief —
not one polished ad, and not variations of one idea. Every ad does two jobs: (1) stop the
scroll in the first 3 seconds, (2) pre-educate the prospect so the sales page does less work.

## The Triple Hook (the opening)
A layered three-part hook. Three EQUAL elements, arrangeable in any order:
1. **Qualifier** — a bold statement that sets the tone and creates anticipation.
2. **Main Statement** — the core value: a specific result or method (use real numbers).
3. **Twist** — the false-belief breaker: destroys the objection and opens a curiosity gap.

Example (core idea: "made $60k in 30 days without going viral"):
"You're not going to believe this, but my business made $60,000 last month — and I did it
without going viral." (Qualifier -> Main -> Twist). The same idea rearranges into 6 hooks;
lead with the Twist for audiences who think they already know the answer.

Rule: the hook must connect to the prospect's pain (their false belief) immediately, and end
on a forward-looking tease — never give away the result up front.

## The 5-part story structure
Every ad runs Hook -> Background -> Conflict -> Resolution -> CTA:
1. **Hook** — Triple Hook; stop the scroll.
2. **Background** — set context, build relatability, establish where they were.
3. **Conflict** — the turning point / what held them back (a relatable struggle).
4. **Resolution** — the transformation and proof (specific numbers, not vague claims).
5. **CTA** — a natural invitation and clear next step. No hard sell.

## False beliefs (the target of every ad)
Every ad should destroy ONE specific false belief. Two kinds:
- **Internal** — what they believe about THEMSELVES ("I can't because ___"): not
  skilled/creative/disciplined enough, too old/young, failed before, no background.
- **External** — what they believe about the WORLD/SOLUTION ("it won't work because ___"):
  too expensive, takes too long, market saturated, need followers/connections/luck, too
  complicated, rigged system.
For a campaign, derive ~10 internal + ~10 external beliefs from the offer, then map each ad
angle to the highest-impact belief it destroys.

## Rules for concept generation
- One angle = one archetype = one false belief. Keep it single-minded.
- Use specific numbers and concrete details; never vague claims.
- Do NOT state an explicit product price in the ad.
- Conversational, punchy, short sentences — not scripted or corporate.
- For a test pack: pick DISTINCT archetypes so angles are genuinely different (a Myth, a
  Data, a Confession, an Underdog — not four flavours of the same idea).

## Notes for AI video production (this app)
- These ads are AI-generated video, not live-shot. The strategy/hook/false-belief layer
  transfers fully; ignore any source guidance about a human videographer or performer.
- On-screen hook text, captions and CTAs are added later in edit (CapCut), not baked into
  generation. Design shots to leave room for text where the hook/CTA will sit.
